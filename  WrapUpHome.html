THE LAST OF US: A BROKEN WORLD

Why this game resonates with me — original reflection (150+ words): The Last of Us isn't just about zombies or fungus; it's about the fragile beauty of human connection. I remember the first time I witnessed Joel hold Ellie's hand after escaping the capitol building — no words, but everything was said. This game taught me that hope can exist even in damp, crumbling ruins. The chemistry between characters, the moral complexity, and the haunting soundtrack by Gustavo Santaolalla feel like a melancholic embrace. I love how every abandoned house tells a silent story: a child's drawing, a suicide note, a birthday banner. It makes you reflect on what you'd sacrifice for those you love. The world is harsh, but the moments of levity — Ellie's terrible puns, the giraffe scene — shine like fireflies in the dark. That's why I built this tribute: to honor Naughty Dog's masterpiece and share the emotional weight that lingers long after the credits roll.
